The Simple Present Tense - To Be


The verb 'to be' is different from other verbs in the simple present tense. Let's look at 'to be' first:


Here's the positive form (positive means a normal sentence, not a negative or a question.)


Positive Positive Short Form
I am tired I'm tired
You are thin You're thin
She, he, it is strong She's strong, he's strong, it's strong
We are serious We're serious
They are funny They're funny


Here's the negative:


Negative Negative Short Form
I am not happy I'm not happy
You are not weak You're not weak
She, he, it is not dumb She's not dumb, he's not dumb, it's not dumb
We are not surprised We're not surprised
They are not bored They're not bored


Let's talk about the question form of the present simple with 'to be'.

Here's the 'yes / no' question form:

  • am I serious?
  • are you smart?
  • is she unhappy?
  • is it far?
  • are we strong
  • are they weak

If you'd like to make a 'wh' question, you just put the question word at the front:


  • Where am I?
  • What are you doing?
  • Why is she fighting?
  • Who is he?
  • When are we leaving?
  • How are they?
